About

I am a PhD candidate in Physics at the University of Genova, supervised by Prof. Paolo Solinas, with collaborative appointments at the Istituto Nazionale di Fisica Nucleare (INFN). My doctoral thesis investigates quantum non-demolition measurement techniques for gradient evaluation and resource reduction in variational quantum algorithms.

My research demonstrates that QNDM-based approaches can significantly reduce measurement shots, circuit executions, and logical gate operations compared to conventional direct measurement techniques — making quantum algorithms more viable on today's NISQ devices.

I have applied these methods to molecular quantum systems including H₂, LiH, and Li₂, using INFN's high-performance computing infrastructure for large-scale hybrid quantum–classical simulations.

Research Projects

Quantum Non-Demolition Measurement Techniques for VQAs

Developed QNDM-based strategies for efficient gradient estimation in variational quantum algorithms. Compared direct measurement (DM) and QNDM protocols for first- and second-order derivative estimation. Demonstrated reduced measurement overhead, logical gate complexity, and circuit execution requirements across molecular Hamiltonians including H₂, LiH, and Li₂.

PhD Research · 2023–2026 INFN Collaboration Qiskit PennyLane Python HPC

Open System Dynamics of Entanglement in Dissipative Environments

Studied entanglement dynamics and decoherence in bipartite quantum systems interacting with thermal, squeezed, and vacuum environments. Developed analytical and simulation-based models for entanglement decay, robustness, and preservation. Part of an HEC-funded research project at COMSATS University Islamabad.

MSc Research · 2018–2019 HEC NRPU Grant Quantum Optics Open Quantum Systems Decoherence
